01Signing in & getting around

Open the website cbdcollege-scheduler.pages.dev in any browser (Chrome, Safari, Edge — all work). The first thing you see is a sign-in box.

How to sign in

1Type your email or your name in the first box.
2Type your password.
3Tap the blue Sign In button.

If you got the password wrong, a red message will appear telling you so. You can try again straight away.

How to sign out

Look for the Sign Out button in the top bar. Tap it to log out.

Switching the colours (dark / light)

The moon 🌙 or sun ☀️ button at the top of the screen swaps between dark mode and light mode. Pick whichever's easier on your eyes — your choice is saved for next time.

What's in the top bar

From left to right (or top to bottom on a phone): your name, a TRAINER badge, then buttons for previewing as an assistant, the bell for messages, exporting a PDF, enabling phone notifications, the dark/light switch, and finally Sign Out.

Saving the app to your phone

On a phone you'll see an 📱 Install button in the top bar. Tap it and follow the prompts — this puts a CBD Scheduler icon on your home screen so it opens like a normal app instead of a website. iPhone users: the screen will show step-by-step pictures for adding the icon.

Turning on phone notifications

Tap the bell-with-text button 🔔 Enable Push in the top bar. Your phone will ask if you want to allow notifications — say yes. After that, your phone will buzz any time a roster changes, even when you don't have the app open.

iPhone owners You have to save the app to your home screen first (the Install step above), then open it from the home-screen icon. Notifications won't arrive if you only use it through Safari.

02How to read the calendar

The five sections

Schedule Classes Staff Notifications Admin

Schedule and Staff each open into two views — the buttons for those appear just under the main row when you tap them:

  • Schedule splits into Per-staff (one person at a time) and Team (everyone).
  • Staff splits into Manage Staff (add / edit people) and Availability (override when someone can work).

What the symbols on each day mean

✓ Green tick = rostered / set up ✕ Red cross = a class is on but you're not Blue ring = today Blue outline = you've selected it

On a computer, each day shows lots of detail — times, student counts, who's working. On a phone, the days shrink down to just the number and a green tick or red cross so they fit. Tap any day to see the full details in a popup.

03Rostering one staff member at a time

This is where you mark a person on or off for a specific day.

First, pick whose roster you're editing

Use the dropdown box labelled Select Staff Member. The calendar will refresh to show their days.

Setting a single day

1Tap the day you want to change.
2A small window pops up. Pick Available, Partial, or Unavailable.
3If you want, also set their start and end time, whether they're the Head Trainer that day, and any notes.
4Tap the green Save button.

The day on the calendar turns green if they're available, yellow if they're partly available, or red if they can't make it. The staff member gets a notification on their phone saying their roster changed.

Removing a day you've already set

Tap the same day, then in the popup tap Remove. The day goes back to blank.

Changing lots of days at once

1Tap several day boxes one after another — they get a blue outline.
2A bar pops up at the bottom of the screen with buttons.
3Tap Available, Partial, or Unavailable to set them all at once — or Clear to wipe them.

Clearing a whole month

The Clear month button removes every entry for that staff member for the month you're looking at. It asks you to confirm first.

The blue card at the top

Above the calendar, the blue Today card shows today's class times, how many students, and who's working. It updates automatically as you change things.

04Seeing who's working — the Team view

A read-only month view showing everyone's roster at once.

What you'll see

Each box lists up to three first names. People with an HT badge next to their name are the Head Trainer for that day. Underneath that you'll see the number of AM and PM students, and an AM CAP or PM CAP label if the class is already full.

To see one day's full details

Tap any day. A popup shows everyone rostered, the class times, and student numbers.

05Setting your weekly class times

This is where you say what time classes normally run each day of the week — Monday's times, Tuesday's times, and so on. The app uses these for every date going forward unless you change a specific date by hand.

How to update a day

1Find the row for the day you want (Mon, Tue, etc.).
2Set the AM class's start and end time.
3Set the PM class's start and end time.
4Type how many students are usually in the AM class, then the PM class.
5Tick the Cap AM or Cap PM box if that session is full to capacity.
6Tap the green Save Defaults button.

Save it quietly, or let staff know?

After tapping Save Defaults you'll see a confirmation with two choices:

  • Save only — saves without telling anyone.
  • Save & Notify — saves and sends a notification only to the staff who are actually affected — i.e. those currently rostered on any upcoming days whose times you just changed.
Example If you change only Wednesday's times, and only Madi and Cameron are rostered on upcoming Wednesdays, only those two get the notification. Nobody else is interrupted. After saving you'll see a toast like "Defaults saved · notified 2 staff".

Quick shortcuts

Three buttons at the top of the section save you time:

  • Copy Mon to Tue–Fri — uses Monday's times for the rest of the weekdays.
  • Copy Sat to Sun — uses Saturday's settings for Sunday too.
  • Copy Mon to all days — overwrites every day with Monday's settings.

06Adding, editing & removing staff

Adding a new staff member

1Tap the green + Add Staff button.
2Type their full name.
3Pick a colour for them — this colour is used everywhere their name appears.
4Tick Head Trainer if they are one. Head Trainers are scheduled differently from assistants.
5Set their pick order number (lower numbers are picked first when the app fills in the roster). Leave blank to put them last.
6If they have an account, choose their username from the Linked user list so they get notifications.
7Tap Save.

Editing or renaming someone

1Find their card in the list.
2Tap the pencil icon on their card.
3Change whatever you need — name, colour, role, pick order, account link.
4Tap Save.

Removing a staff member

1Find their card and tap the trash-can icon.
2Confirm. This removes their past roster days too, and it can't be undone.
Be careful Deleting someone wipes their roster history. If you just want them off future rosters, leave them in place and either mark them unavailable or give them a high pick number.

Connecting a staff member to a user account

For someone to see their own roster and get notifications, their staff record needs to be linked to their sign-in account. Edit them, choose their account from the Linked user list, and save.

07Setting pick order, start times & rules

Pick Order by Day

Lower numbers get picked first when the app fills in the roster. Head Trainers aren't on this list — they're handled separately.

1At the top of the Pick Order card, tap the day you want to set (Mon, Tue, etc.).
2Drag people up or down, or type a number for each.
3Tick Rotate priority each [day] if you want two assistants to take turns being picked first.
4Tap Save This Day.

The two shortcut buttons let you copy the current order to the rest of the week: Apply to Tue–Fri or Apply to all days.

Overriding someone's availability

Tap Staff at the top, then Availability in the pill bar that appears underneath.

1Pick the assistant.
2Tap a day on their calendar.
3Choose Available or Not Available. Add a note if you like.
4Tap Save. They get a notification.
5To undo, open the same day and tap Clear.

Assistant Start Times by Slot

When the app picks assistants automatically, the first one picked gets the "Slot 1" start time, the second one gets "Slot 2", and so on. This grid lets you set what time each slot starts. Change any time and tap Save Start Times — same Save or Save & Notify choice as before.

Who gets notified Only assistants rostered on upcoming dates whose class start time matches a changed slot group. E.g. if you change the 08:00-AM-class slot times, only assistants on upcoming days where the AM class starts at 08:00 are pinged.

Auto-roster Rules

Two settings here decide how many assistants the app picks:

  • Need 1 assistant when class ≥ N — the smallest class size that needs at least one assistant.
  • Need 2 assistants when class ≥ N — the class size at which a second assistant is needed.
  • Who works the first Saturday of each month? — pick which Head Trainer kicks off the weekend rotation. The other Head Trainer takes Sunday, and they swap each following weekend.

Tap Save Rules — same Save or Save & Notify choice.

Who gets notified If you change the assistant-count thresholds, only assistants rostered on upcoming dates are notified (their pick spot might shift). If you change which Head Trainer kicks off the weekend, only the two Head Trainers are notified.

08Sending a message to staff

The Notifications tab lets you send a custom message — to everyone or to one specific person.

How to send

1Tap Notifications in the top bar.
2Use the dropdown to choose who gets it — All staff or one person's name.
3Type a short title (like "New roster posted").
4Type the message.
5Tap the green Send Notification button.
6Check the preview, then tap Send.
What happens next The people you sent to will see a number on the bell icon. If they've turned on phone notifications, their phone will also buzz.

Reading messages sent to you

Tap the bell 🔔 in the top bar. A list of recent messages appears. Tap any message to mark it as read.

09Creating user accounts

The Admin tab is where you add, edit, and delete login accounts.

Adding a new account

1Tap + Create User.
2Enter their email and a temporary password.
3Pick whether they're a Trainer or an Assistant.
4Save. Pass the email and temporary password on to them — they can change it later.

Editing or deleting

Each row has a pencil icon to edit the person's name or role, and a trash icon to delete the account.

Don't forget After creating a user account, go to the Staff tab and link the new account to that person's staff entry — otherwise they won't get their notifications.

10Looking up one day's details

Tap any day on any calendar to bring up everything the app knows about that day.

You'll see the class times, the AM and PM student count, whether the class is full, and the names of everyone rostered (with the Head Trainer at the top). Trainers can also change the class times for that specific date from this screen, overriding the weekly default.

Who gets notified when you change one date's times Only the staff who are actually rostered on that day. Everyone else is left alone — no spammy "the times changed for a day I'm not even on" notifications.

11Loading a roster from an Excel file

If you already have a roster spreadsheet, the app can read it in instead of you typing each day by hand.

How to do it

1On the Schedule tab, tap Import Roster.
2Tap Choose file and pick your .xlsx or .csv file.
3A preview appears, listing each day the app found. Yellow warnings show where the app couldn't match something (like a name it didn't recognise).
4Untick any days you don't want to load in.
5Tap Apply Import. Confirm, and the roster is saved.
File format Your spreadsheet needs to match the layout the app expects — the one with rows labelled ABS 3 hrs am, ABS 2 hrs Art, Trainer 2, and Total # of students in the first column.

12Letting the app fill in the roster for you

Once people have set their availability, the app can pick who works each day automatically.

How to do it

1Schedule tab → tap Auto-roster.
2A preview shows every day that would change. Green = adding someone, red = removing.
3Untick any days you don't want changed.
4Tap Apply.

The app respects these things when picking:

  • The pick order you set on the Staff tab.
  • The class size rules (Auto-roster Rules).
  • Full classes — it won't add an assistant if the AM or PM is capped.
  • The Head Trainer weekend rotation.
  • Each staff member's availability — nobody is picked for a day they marked unavailable.

13Saving a roster as a PDF

1Tap Export PDF in the top bar.
2Choose the month range (it starts on the month you're looking at).
3Choose which staff to include (everyone by default).
4Tap Export. The PDF saves to your computer or phone.

You can print the PDF, email it, or save it for your records.

14Notifications — what's sent and when

Two places notifications show up

  1. The bell icon at the top of the app. A red number tells you how many you haven't read yet. Tap it to see them.
  2. Your phone's notifications — if you tapped Enable Push earlier, these arrive even when the app isn't open.

What triggers a notification

Notifications are always targeted to the staff actually affected — never a blanket message to everyone unless you choose that from the Notifications tab.

When this happensWho gets notified
You change one staff member's roster on a dayThat staff member only
You override an assistant's availability for a dayThat assistant only
You change one specific date's class times (in the day-detail popup)Only the staff rostered on that day
You save Default Class Times with "Save & Notify"Only staff rostered on upcoming dates whose day-of-week you changed (next 90 days)
You save Slot Times with "Save & Notify"Only assistants rostered on upcoming dates whose AM start matches a changed slot group
You save Auto-roster Rules — assistant count thresholdsOnly assistants rostered on upcoming dates (next 60 days)
You save Auto-roster Rules — first Saturday Head TrainerOnly the two Head Trainers
You send a message from the Notifications tabWhoever you chose (one person or All staff)
Why this matters Staff only hear from the app about changes that touch them personally. Nobody gets pinged about a Tuesday-only change if they only ever work Sundays.

If a staff member says they didn't get a notification

  • Check their staff record is linked to a user account (Staff tab → edit them → check the Linked user field).
  • Ask them if they tapped the Enable Push button on their phone.
  • iPhone users have to install the app to their home screen first, then turn on notifications. They won't work from Safari.
  • The bell on their screen works either way — ask them to check that too.

15Previewing what an assistant sees

Sometimes you want to check what the roster looks like from a particular assistant's point of view — before sending it out.

1Tap View as Assistant in the top bar.
2The screen switches to the assistant tabs (My Roster and My Availability) and shows a bar where you can pick which person to preview.
3Pick a name. The screen now shows their view exactly as they'd see it.
4When you're done, tap Back to Trainer View in the top bar.

16If something isn't working

A staff member says they didn't get a notification

  • Their account needs to be linked to their staff record. Edit them on the Staff tab and check.
  • They need to tap the Enable Push button on their phone, and tap Allow when their phone asks.
  • iPhone users need to install the app to their home screen first.
  • Even without phone notifications, the bell icon in the app always works.

The Schedule calendar shows blank cells

  • You probably haven't picked a person from the dropdown yet.
  • If the person is new and has no roster days set, every cell will be empty until you add some (manually, by importing, or by running auto-roster).

The default times aren't being used for a specific date

A date you set by hand always wins over the weekly default. If you want it to use the default again, tap that date and clear the times you typed in by hand.

Phone notifications aren't working on iPhone

iPhones only deliver app notifications after the app's been installed to the home screen. Tap the Install button, follow the steps to add the app to your home screen, then open it from the home screen icon and tap Enable Push.
